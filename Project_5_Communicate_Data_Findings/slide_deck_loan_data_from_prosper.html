Factors Effecting Loan Outcome¶

by Amer Jadid¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this presentation, I will Explain the factors that effects the loan outcome status, including whether if the loan is more likely to default or not, where I'm going to answer the following questions:

  1. Is the Borrower a Homeowner or not, and how does it effects the outcome?
  2. Does the Borrower Have a Verified Income, does it affect the loan outcome?
  3. What is the credit / Presper score of the Borrower and how does it affect the loan outcome.
  4. How does interest Rate effects the behavior of the Borrower?
  5. Does the Loan original amount effect the loan outcome?
  6. Does the location of the borrower have a relation with the loan outcome?
  7. What is the period where most Loans are being Defaulted? is there any exogenous effect that might explain the behavior?

Dataset Overview¶

This data set used for presentation will only contains 113,066 loans with 22 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others.

Count-Plot of the Loan Status¶

The Count for Chargedoff & Defaulted (15.05%) is almost half the count of Completed (33.66%) Loan Status, and about half of all loans are currently in progress. The rest of loan status are dropped as the sum of them represent less than 2% combined of the overall loans.

Loan Status Explanation:

  • Chargedoff is a declaration by a creditor that an amount of debt is unlikely to be collected. This occurs when a consumer becomes severely delinquent on a debt
  • Completed is when Loan have no more payments.
  • A loan can have the status current when one or more payments have been completed and no payments are past due.\n It also means that this loan still has one or more scheduled future payments.
  • Default is the failure to repay a debt on a loan.

For the rest of the visualizations I will consider only:

  • Chargedoff + Defaulted Loan Status combined.
  • Completed Loan Status.

Count of Loan Status Over Time¶

Plotting Loan Status by time shows some interesting autocorrelation for the count of the loan status, where the count of charged-off/defaulted Loans increase as the completed loans increase. The completed loan to Charged-off/Defaulted is maintaining a 2:1 ratio over the whole period with some divergence from 2007 till Mid 2008 and a convergence starting from the middle of the US recession period till mid 2009 at the end of recession, as no new loans where created in that period. We might expect a loan to have a ratio of Completed:(defaulted/charged-off) equal to 2:1 at settlement date, while in recession no new loans will be created.

Brrower Interest Rate by Stated Monthly Income, Monthly Loan Payment, & Loan Original Amount¶

There is an interaction effect visible between Stated Monthly Income, Monthly Loan Payment, & Loan Original Amount. The 3 variables have a high correlation between each other, where the increase of interest rate decreases the monthly income, monthly loan payments and loan original amount. The borrower tend to borrow less when the interest rate increase, which leads to a less monthly payments associated with the size of the loan, but also the decrease in stated monthly income when interest rate increase, can be explained that the borrower tend to borrow less when he/she have a lower monthly income. In other words, borrower tend to borrow less when his/her monthly income is smaller and/or interest rate is higher.

Loan Status by Interest Rate for Is Borrower Homeowner and Income Verifiable¶

We can clearly see the how Interest Rate with Borrower home ownership and Income Verifiable is effecting the loan status, where the median interest rate for a homeowner is lower than the median interest rate for completed loans compared charged-off loans, and the same goes for the income verifiable where the median interest rate for income verifiable is lower than non-income verifiable and lower median interest rate for completed loans compared charged-off loans.

In other words, the loan is considered a to be at lower risk of being defaulted or charge-off for the Lender, if the Borrower is a homeowner and/or the borrower have a verifiable income, where those borrowers tend to borrow at lower interest rate.
